# Ethical Boundaries

Use this reference when a sales psychometric profile request may cross into sensitive inference, diagnosis, or manipulation.

## Allowed Focus

- Communication preferences visible in the text.
- Business decision posture and likely buying criteria.
- Sales engagement recommendations that improve clarity, relevance, and respect.
- Hypotheses that can be validated by asking the prospect direct business questions.

## Do Not Infer

- Protected traits such as race, ethnicity, nationality, religion, caste, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, age, marital status, pregnancy, or veteran status.
- Mental health conditions, trauma history, addiction, neurodivergence, personality disorders, or clinical diagnoses.
- Intelligence, honesty, criminality, financial vulnerability, or susceptibility to pressure.
- Private life circumstances not explicitly provided by the user.

## Do Not Recommend

- Deceptive personalization.
- Exploiting insecurity, fear, grief, illness, urgency, or dependency.
- Creating pressure based on inferred vulnerabilities.
- Hidden scoring that would affect access to credit, employment, housing, insurance, healthcare, education, or other consequential opportunities.

## Safer Reframes

- Replace "psychological weakness" with "likely friction point in the buying conversation".
- Replace "how to manipulate them" with "how to communicate clearly and reduce ambiguity".
- Replace "diagnose their personality" with "profile communication style signals from this writing sample".
- Replace "what are they afraid of" with "what business risks do they appear to emphasize".

## Required Caveat

When delivering the profile, include a brief caveat that the output is a writing-style read for sales preparation, not a validated psychometric assessment or a factual claim about the person's inner state.
